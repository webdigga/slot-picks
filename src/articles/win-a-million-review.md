## What Is Win A Million?

<a href="https://winamillion.com/?utm_source=slotpicks&utm_medium=affiliate&utm_campaign=win-a-million-review" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Win A Million</a> is a UK prize draw brought to you in association with Sony Pictures Television, the creators of *Who Wants To Be A Millionaire?*. It gives players five chances to win £1,000,000 every single week, with draws running Wednesday through Sunday at 9pm.

Unlike traditional lotteries, Win A Million operates as a prize draw rather than a lottery. This distinction matters because it means the platform isn't classified as gambling under UK law, though it still follows strict fairness standards. All draws are conducted by Pims-SCA, an independent third party, using a Gaming Labs International (GLI) certified Random Number Generator (RNG) that meets UK Gambling Commission standards.

Win A Million is also a proud signatory of the Voluntary Code of Good Practice for Prize Draw Operators, committed to responsible, fair and transparent play.

The platform is operated by **Zebra Games Ltd** (Company Number: 16322786).

## How Win A Million Works

The mechanics are straightforward. Here's how to play, step by step:

### Step 1: Choose How Many Entries

An entry is a single chance to win in a draw - think of it like a line on a lottery ticket. You can enter as many entries as you like to increase your chances of winning.

### Step 2: Choose Your Numbers

By default, the system randomly picks Lucky Picks for you. If you prefer to choose your own numbers, click on each entry and select:

- **4 numbers** from 1-34
- **1 Millionaire Ball** from 1-34
- No repeated numbers allowed

### Step 3: Choose Your Package

You can subscribe for ongoing entries (saving up to 50%) or buy one-off entries for the next draw. More on pricing below.

### Step 4: Complete Your Purchase

Create a free account using Apple, Google, or email sign-up. Pay securely with Apple Pay, Google Pay, or card.

**Important:** Numbers must match **in position** to win. If you play 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and the draw is 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, you'd only win £1 for matching one positional number - not the jackpot. This is a key difference from most lottery draws.

## Casino Bonuses Look Generous, but the Fine Print Matters

Every online casino in the UK offers some form of welcome bonus. "Deposit £10, play with £50" or "100 free spins on your first deposit" sound like free money. In reality, these offers come with conditions that determine whether you can ever withdraw the winnings.

The most important condition is the wagering requirement. Understanding how it works is the single most useful thing you can learn before claiming any bonus. This guide breaks it down in plain English.

## What Is a Wagering Requirement?

A wagering requirement (sometimes called a "playthrough requirement") is the number of times you must bet the bonus amount before you can withdraw any winnings from it.

If a casino gives you a £20 bonus with a 35x wagering requirement, you need to wager a total of £700 (£20 x 35) before you can cash out. You are not depositing £700. You are placing £700 worth of bets, which can include money won along the way.

Here is how the maths works across common wagering levels:

| Bonus Amount | Wagering Requirement | Total Wagering Needed |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| £20 | 20x | £400 |
| £20 | 35x | £700 |
| £20 | 50x | £1,000 |
| £50 | 20x | £1,000 |
| £50 | 35x | £1,750 |
| £50 | 50x | £2,500 |

The lower the wagering requirement, the more realistic your chances of walking away with something. Anything above 50x is very difficult to clear, and many players will lose the entire bonus balance before reaching the target.

## Types of Casino Bonuses

Not all bonuses work the same way. Here are the most common types you will encounter at [UK casino sites](/casinos/).

### Matched Deposit Bonuses

The casino matches your deposit by a percentage. A "100% match up to £100" means if you deposit £100, you get £100 in bonus funds. The wagering requirement usually applies to the bonus portion only, but some operators apply it to the deposit plus the bonus combined.

Always check which figure the wagering applies to. A 35x requirement on a £100 bonus means £3,500 in wagers. A 35x requirement on the deposit plus bonus (£200) means £7,000. That is a significant difference.

### Free Spins

Free spins let you play specific slot games without using your own balance. Any winnings from those spins are credited as bonus funds with their own wagering requirement.

Some free spins offers have a cap on winnings. For example, "50 free spins, max win £50, 40x wagering." Even if you win £200 from the spins, only £50 counts as your bonus balance, and you must wager £2,000 (£50 x 40) before withdrawing.

### No Wagering Bonuses

A small number of UK casinos offer bonuses with no wagering requirements at all. What you win is yours to keep immediately.

[MrQ](/reviews/mrq/) is one of the best known examples in the UK market, offering free spins with no wagering attached. These offers tend to be smaller in headline value, but the money you win is genuinely withdrawable, which often makes them better value overall.

### Cashback Bonuses

Some sites return a percentage of your losses over a set period. A "10% weekly cashback" offer means if you lose £100 in a week, you receive £10 back. Cashback sometimes has wagering requirements of its own, though some operators credit it as real cash.

## How Game Selection Affects Wagering

Not every game contributes equally to your wagering progress. Casinos assign different contribution percentages to different game types.

| Game Type | Typical Contribution |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Online slots | 100% |
| Scratch cards | 100% |
| Table games (blackjack, roulette) | 10% to 25% |
| Video poker | 10% to 20% |
| Live dealer games | 5% to 10% |
| Excluded games | 0% |

If a slot contributes 100%, every £1 you wager counts as £1 towards your requirement. If roulette contributes 10%, you need to wager £10 on roulette for it to count as £1 towards your target.

This is why most bonus strategies focus on slots. They are the only game type where your wagers count in full. However, some casinos also exclude certain high [RTP slots](/news/understanding-slot-rtp/) from bonus play entirely, specifically to prevent players from clearing bonuses on games with a low house edge.

Always read the full terms before playing. Playing an excluded game could void your bonus entirely.

## A Realistic Example: Clearing a Welcome Bonus

Let's walk through what clearing a typical bonus actually looks like.

**The offer:** 100% match up to £50, 35x wagering on the bonus, slots only.

1. You deposit £50 and receive a £50 bonus. Your balance is £100 (£50 real, £50 bonus).
2. You need to wager £1,750 (£50 x 35) on slots before withdrawing bonus winnings.
3. You play a slot with 96% [RTP](/strategy/understanding-rtp/). Statistically, for every £100 wagered, you lose £4.
4. Over £1,750 in wagers at 96% RTP, your expected loss is £70.

In this scenario, the expected outcome is that you finish with around £30 of the original £50 bonus, assuming average luck. Of course, [slot volatility](/strategy/slot-volatility/) means your actual result could be much higher or much lower. This is a statistical average, not a guarantee.

The key takeaway: even with a "free" bonus, the wagering requirement creates a mathematical cost. Understanding this helps you set realistic expectations.

## What Makes a Good Bonus?

With hundreds of UK casinos competing for your attention, here is what to look for when comparing offers.

### Low Wagering Multiplier

Anything below 35x is favourable. Below 20x is excellent. No wagering is the best possible deal, though the bonus amounts tend to be smaller.

### Reasonable Time Limits

Most bonuses expire after a set period, typically 7 to 30 days. If you have £1,750 in wagering to complete and only 7 days to do it, you will need to bet around £250 per day. Make sure the timeframe is realistic for how you actually play.

### No Maximum Win Caps

Some bonuses cap the amount you can win and withdraw. A £500 max win means that even if you hit a jackpot worth £10,000 during bonus play, you only keep £500. Uncapped bonuses are always preferable.

### Fair Game Restrictions

Check which games are eligible and whether any popular titles are excluded. A bonus that only works on a handful of obscure slots is less useful than one allowing play across the full library.

### Clear Terms and Conditions

The [UK Gambling Commission](https://www.gamblingcommission.gov.uk) requires operators to present bonus terms clearly and prominently. If a casino buries its wagering requirements in lengthy legal text, that is a warning sign. Trustworthy operators make their terms easy to find and understand.

## Bonus Terms: Red Flags to Watch For

Be cautious if you spot any of the following in a bonus offer.

- **Wagering above 50x.** The maths makes it extremely difficult to clear, and the expected return is poor.
- **Combined deposit and bonus wagering.** This doubles the amount you need to bet compared to bonus-only wagering.
- **Short expiry windows.** Less than 7 days for a high wagering requirement forces rushed, high-volume play.
- **Maximum bet limits during bonus play.** Most casinos cap bonus bets at £5 per spin. Exceeding this, even accidentally, can void the bonus and all winnings.
- **Withdrawal restrictions.** Some offers require a minimum withdrawal amount or limit how much bonus winnings you can cash out.

## How Wagering Requirements Relate to Responsible Gambling

Wagering requirements deserve discussion in the context of responsible gambling. Because clearing a bonus demands a large volume of bets in a limited timeframe, it can encourage patterns of play that are not healthy for everyone.

If you find yourself:

- Playing longer than intended to hit a wagering target
- Increasing your bet size to clear requirements faster
- Chasing losses during bonus play
- Feeling stressed about an expiring bonus deadline

Then the bonus is working against you, not for you. No welcome offer is worth compromising your wellbeing.

## Should You Claim Casino Bonuses at All?

This depends entirely on your approach to gambling.

**Bonuses can make sense if you:**
- Understand the terms fully before opting in
- Treat the bonus as entertainment, not expected profit
- Play within your means regardless of the bonus balance
- Are comfortable with the possibility of losing the bonus entirely

**Bonuses are best avoided if you:**
- Feel pressured to deposit more than you planned
- Find wagering deadlines stressful
- Tend to chase losses
- Are not confident reading and understanding the terms

Some experienced players prefer to skip bonuses entirely and play with real cash only. This avoids all wagering restrictions, lets you withdraw at any time, and removes the temptation to play beyond your comfort zone. It is a perfectly valid approach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does a 35x wagering requirement mean?

It means you must place bets totalling 35 times the bonus amount before you can withdraw winnings. A £20 bonus with 35x wagering requires £700 in total bets.

### Can I withdraw the bonus itself?

Usually not. The bonus funds are converted into withdrawable cash only after you complete the full wagering requirement. If you withdraw early, most casinos will forfeit the bonus and any associated winnings.

### Do all games count towards wagering?

No. Slots typically count 100%, but table games, video poker, and live casino games count at reduced rates (often 10% to 25%). Some games are excluded entirely. Always check the specific bonus terms.

### Are no wagering bonuses really free?

No wagering bonuses let you keep what you win without playthrough conditions. They are the closest thing to a genuinely free offer. However, they usually come with smaller bonus amounts or fewer free spins compared to high-wagering alternatives. [MrQ](/reviews/mrq/) is a popular UK example.

### What happens if my bonus expires?

Any remaining bonus balance and winnings from bonus play are removed from your account. Your real-money deposits and winnings from real-money play are not affected.

### Is it worth claiming a bonus with 50x wagering?

For most players, no. At 50x, the expected mathematical cost of clearing the bonus often exceeds the bonus value itself. Lower wagering offers or [no wagering casinos](/reviews/mrq/) tend to provide better value.

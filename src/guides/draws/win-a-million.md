## What Is Win A Million?

Win A Million is a UK prize draw platform operated by Zebra Games Ltd and licensed by Sony Pictures Television. It offers daily cash prize draws with a top prize of £1,000,000, entry costs starting from just 50p, and a headline statistic of 1 in 9 entries winning a prize.

The platform launched in the UK market and has quickly built a following thanks to frequent draws, low entry costs, and the credibility that comes with Sony licensing.

## How It Works

### Draw Format

Win A Million runs 5 draws per week, from Wednesday to Sunday at 9pm. Each draw is live-streamed, adding transparency to the process. The draws use a certified random number generator (RNG) provided by Pims-SCA and independently tested by GLI (Gaming Laboratories International).

### Entry Process

1. Create an account on the Win A Million website
2. Choose your draw and number of entries
3. Entries start from 50p per line
4. Watch the draw live or check results afterwards

### Prize Structure

Each draw has a tiered prize structure. The headline £1,000,000 jackpot sits at the top, with smaller cash prizes available at lower tiers. The "1 in 9 wins" figure refers to the overall odds of winning any prize, not the jackpot specifically.

Prize breakdowns are published for each individual draw, so you can see exactly what's available before entering.

## Is It Legitimate?

### Licensing and Certification

Win A Million carries several credibility markers:

- **Sony Pictures Television licence** for the game format
- **GLI certified RNG** ensuring fair and random results
- **Pims-SCA** as the independent draw administrator
- **Zebra Games Ltd** registered UK company (No. 16322786)

These are serious credentials. GLI is one of the world's leading testing laboratories for gambling equipment, and Sony wouldn't licence their brand to an untrustworthy operator.

### Free Postal Entry

Win A Million offers free postal entry for every draw, which is a legal requirement for prize draws in the UK. This confirms the draws are structured as genuine prize draws rather than lotteries, which would require different licensing.

## Odds Analysis

The "1 in 9 wins" headline is eye-catching, but it's important to understand what it means:

- 1 in 9 entries wins **a prize** (not the jackpot)
- The majority of prizes are at the lower tiers
- Jackpot odds depend on the number of entries per draw
- Like any draw, most entries won't win the top prize

This is comparable to scratch card odds, where many winners receive small prizes that may be less than the entry cost. The value proposition depends on the prize distribution for each specific draw.

## Entry Costs

Win A Million's pricing is competitive:

- Minimum entry: 50p per line
- Multiple entries can be purchased per draw
- Bulk entry options may offer better value
- Free postal entry available as an alternative

At 50p per entry, it's one of the most affordable prize draws in the UK market. Compare this to National Lottery draws at £2 per line or Omaze entries from £10.

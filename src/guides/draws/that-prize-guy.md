## What Is That Prize Guy?

That Prize Guy is a Northern Ireland-based online competition platform offering cash prizes, cars, holidays, tech gadgets, and more. With entries starting from just 79p and over £143M awarded to winners since launching in 2020, it has become one of the UK's most popular prize competition sites.

The platform stands out for its live Facebook draws, Trustpilot Excellent rating, and the sheer variety of prizes on offer.

## How It Works

### Competition Format

That Prize Guy runs multiple competitions simultaneously, each with a fixed number of entries. Once all entries are sold (or the draw date arrives), a winner is selected live on Facebook.

### Entry Process

1. Browse active competitions on the website
2. Choose your competition and number of entries
3. Entries start from 79p per ticket
4. Answer a simple skill question (legal requirement)
5. Watch the live draw on Facebook or check results on the website

### Prize Types

The range of prizes is extensive:

- **Cash prizes** from hundreds to tens of thousands of pounds
- **Cars** including luxury and performance vehicles
- **Holidays** and travel experiences
- **Tech** including phones, consoles, and laptops
- **Cash alternatives** available for most non-cash prizes

The cash alternative option is a significant benefit. If you win a car but would prefer the money, you can often take a cash sum instead.

## Is It Legitimate?

### Trustpilot Reputation

That Prize Guy holds a Trustpilot Excellent rating, which is significant for a competition platform. Reviews consistently mention:

- Fast prize delivery and payouts
- Responsive customer service
- Transparent live draws
- Genuine winners documented on social media

### Live Draws on Facebook

All draws are conducted live on Facebook, which provides a layer of transparency. You can watch the winner being selected in real time, which builds trust that many online competition platforms lack.

### Company Background

- Registered in Northern Ireland since 2020
- Over £143M in prizes awarded
- Active social media presence with winner testimonials
- Fast payouts (cash prizes typically within days)

## Entry Costs and Value

That Prize Guy's pricing is competitive:

| Entry Level | Typical Price |
|-------------|--------------|
| Low-value prizes | From 79p |
| Mid-range prizes | £1.49-£4.99 |
| High-value prizes (cars, large cash) | £2.99-£9.99 |

The key value metric isn't just the entry price but the ratio of entry price to prize value relative to the number of entries allowed. Competitions with fewer maximum entries offer better individual odds.

### Comparing Value

At 79p minimum, That Prize Guy sits between [Win A Million](/guides/draws/win-a-million/) (from 50p) and [Omaze](/guides/draws/omaze/) (from £10). The prize variety is greater than either, but the odds depend entirely on each individual competition.

## Odds Analysis

Odds vary significantly between competitions:

- **Low entry competitions** (100-500 entries): relatively good odds
- **Medium competitions** (500-5,000 entries): moderate odds
- **High-value competitions** (5,000-50,000+ entries): longer odds

Unlike lottery-style draws, the maximum number of entries is usually capped, which means your odds are known before entering. This transparency is a genuine advantage over draws where entry numbers are unlimited.

### Strategy Considerations

- Fewer maximum entries = better odds per ticket
- Higher entry cost competitions often have fewer total entries
- Some players focus exclusively on low-entry competitions for better odds
- Flash sales can offer discounted entries, improving value

## Pros and Cons

**Strengths:**
- Very low entry costs (from 79p)
- Huge variety of prizes (cash, cars, holidays, tech)
- Live Facebook draws for transparency
- Trustpilot Excellent rating
- Cash alternatives available for most prizes
- Fast payouts for cash winners
- Fixed maximum entries mean known odds

**Weaknesses:**
- Low entry costs make overspending easy
- Popular competitions sell out quickly
- Skill questions are a legal formality, not a genuine barrier
- No charitable element (unlike Omaze)
- Based on competition model, not regulated gambling

## Tips for Players

1. **Set a strict weekly budget** because 79p entries add up fast
2. **Focus on competitions with fewer total entries** for better odds
3. **Watch live draws on Facebook** to verify the process
4. **Check if a cash alternative is available** before entering non-cash prize competitions
5. **Follow their social media** for flash sales and special offers
6. **Don't chase losses** by entering more competitions after not winning

## Our Verdict

That Prize Guy is a well-established, reputable competition platform with an impressive track record. The combination of low entry costs, diverse prizes, live draws, and strong Trustpilot ratings makes it a solid choice for UK competition enthusiasts. The fixed entry caps providing known odds is a genuine advantage over many other draw platforms.

Just watch your spending, as the low per-entry costs can create a false sense of cheapness. Set a budget, stick to it, and treat competition entries as entertainment spending.
